Valorant Lineups

In late 2019, after the announcement of Valorant Lineups by Riot Games. I had thought about the importance of third-party community created applications that were instrumental in learning my experience in Counter-Strike Global-Offensive. Without these expertly created community driven tools I wouldn't be anywhere near as proficient in the tactical shooter as I am today. I wanted to be able to allow other players of Valorant, have the same experiences I had in learning the game. However, many sites that offer strategies or 'tips'. Are very heavily curated and are often not community-driven. Thus, Valorant Lineups was born.

Valorant Datamining

In order to aquire resources for my web app 'Valorant Lineups', I had to procure sound files from the game itself. Since then I've been looking at various ways in extracting these files from the game. Various avenues exist in the aims to extract the .pak files Unreal uses as compression methods for games designed in their engine. FModel is a very simple and deep tooling which allows effective data-mining of any Unreal based game. Using this I've created Python scripts to read the JSON architecture. This process was simple to write in Python and distribute online via YouTube guide and Google Drive.

A Game of NATS

During my second year at the University of the West of Scotland, we were all presented as computing students a chance to enter the competition presented to us by the National Air Traffic Service. We had two weeks to envision a game which educated interested individuals about air traffic controls. Any approach was encouraged, blue sky thinking, basic design documentation, or full blown development. I decided to fully develop a prototype game within the two week timeframe, and won a prize for it!
