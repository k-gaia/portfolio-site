Bachelor of Computer Science with Honours

My time at the University of the West of Scotland gave me a deep understanding in what it means to work in Computing Science. The teamwork required, the vast set of skills you must dip into, and the passion you have to hone and work at it for years not just before and after university, but during too. My first two years at UWS was much more noticeably 'easier' than my latter years, these years were most certainly used for settling me into the University mindset and for being such a young university, I was given good advice and guidance when required, as well as given a clear understanding of how the universities systems worked.

I developed a deep understanding of developing various applications in a myraid of programming languages such as Java, C++, Rust, Python, and C#. Given a really in-depth knowledge of 'exotic' systems to layman such as unix based layouts. I truly had no idea how Linux and its distributions worked, and honestly the prospect of learning something as complicated as Linux can be was daunting. But under the tutelage of the lecturers here at UWS, I now run my own media/game server in pure Debian built from the ground up. Once they had instilled the core of how the operating system works, it really became clear. Amongst other teachings, I learned how various functions of computers work not limited to operating systems, but how various data systems and memory structures operate at the kernel level.

During my penultimate year at UWS, I continued developing a passion project known as Valorant Lineups I had developed a front-end prototype the year before for. The staff at UWS had no issues with this and encouraged me to push forward with this idea and formalise it into a fully realised web application. Under the supervision of a lecturer who had judged me at a student competition for the National Air Traffic Service, I was able to complete a prototype for, and still am developing Valorant Lineups. My time at the University of the West of Scotland has made me keenly interested in studying a masters related to Computing, or diversifying and studying a completely new field entirely. I had my initial doubts and nerves about university, but UWS showed me that furthering your education formally is never a bad thing. Especially when funded by the Scottish Government!

Fourth Year - SCQF Level 10

Module Description Grade / GPA
Human Computer Interaction Learning HCI principles of design, in practice and formally co-draft an appliaction utilising a software planning package. B1, 2.5
Machine Learning A thorough examination of basic machine learning principles and techniques, from data pre-processing, to generation of sophisticated machine learning models and proper useage of said models. A2, 3.5
Secure Programming Standardised programming enabling secure development. Examination of novel code identifying basic problems in violation of SEI CERT standards. Design of a program in which has secure memory management via Rust. A1, 4.0
Data Warehouse Environment Thorough examination of how data is structured throughout an organisation. Examining how business practices link to various computing processes enabling business mobility and decision making. Basic data visualiation and exploration. A3, 3.0
Honours Project Valorant Lineups. A mapping application designed to enable players of Valorant to easily share and critique strategies on the fly, be it in or out of game. TBA, TBA

Third Year - SCQF Level 9

Module Description Grade / GPA
Internet Scripting Enriching web development skills, examining various MVC website architectures and various server handlers provide such architectures. This module primarily was taught in Microsoft Razor (C#). A3, 3.0
Computer Game AI A complete breakdown in game theory, pathfinding algorithims, and game engine principality. Written in C++, students must integrate various pathfinding principles into a custom written game engine. A1, 4.0
Information Security Management A formalised look into how data is treated legally by corporations and governments alike. How users can protect themselves on the web, and how to identify various bad actors through risk management and pen testing. A3, 3.0
Research Methods in Computing A prepatory module for writing a formalised literature review in preperation for our penultimate honours project dissertation. A3, 3.0
Unix System Administration A complete guide on how to administer a Unix based system. Assessed on code snippets, shell-scripts, and a live code demonstration of a system setup. B1, 2.5
Database Applications A more advanced design of database applications and integrating various pieces of software together to manage effectively university data. A2, 3.5
Professional Computing Practice A look into the required laws and ethics behind professionally practicing computing as a trade. Enhancing employability prospects and taking a step back from development a little. A3, 3.0

Second Year - SCQF Level 8

Module Description Grade / GPA
Structures and Algorithims Examining thoroughly, data structures and algorithims, a necessity for everday development within any programming language. These principles were developed first pimarily within Java. Stacks, Queues, Binary Searches, Linked Lists, Search Trees. A3, 3.0
WBL 2 - Group Project A simple research and design project aimed at introducing students to proper research work, design work, and presentation work throughout the whole term. B1, 2.5
Programming for Mobile Devices Introduction to programming devices for Android and IOS. This module in particular used Android Studio, I developed a simple native app version of a website we had developed for first year students. A3, 3.0
Database Development An introductory module aimed at developing students core database skills, fluency in SQL, protocols for managing a database, and designing and implementing a database system from the ground up in MSSQL: Microsoft SQL and SQL Server. A3, 3.0
Object Oriented Analysis Introduction to proper OOP principles, crossover with Structures and Algorithims. Explaining core data structures, and algorithims integrating recursion, big O notation, and source control. Represented all through UML and basic data flow diagraming. B1, 2.5
Operating Systems A more in-depth look into how various operating systems work. Things like how files are managed, memory is stored within RAM and accessed differently across operating systems, paging files and the like. Various computer architecure sprinkled in when required. A2, 3.0
