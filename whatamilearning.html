Personal Study

Recently I've been leaning into more cybersecurity news and taking an interest in how code can be malicious. Learning languages and software development principles is as all well and good but varying it up can be very interesting especially when flipping established thinking on its head.

I'm always learning more about shell-scripting and Linux based operations with a home server used for home automation, media serving, and game-server hosting. Its fun to have your own playground to put stuff up, try and fail, and then put your own fires out.

Moving forward, I want to learn more about game-design too, making my own minigames in Unity or Unreal is fun for an evening, its really tricky but rewarding when you piece things together.
